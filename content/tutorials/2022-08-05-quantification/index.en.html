The different quantification methods

Yuan et al. Quantifying Short-Chain Chlorinated Paraffin Congener Groups (Environ. Sci. Technol. 2017, 51, 10633−10641)

The response factor can be calculated using: \[\begin{equation} RF(C_nCl_m) = \frac{Area(C_nCl_m)}{Conc(C_nCl_m)} \tag{1} \end{equation}\]

The relative amount of \(C_nCl_m\) in a \(C_n\) reference standard can be described by the Gaussian distribution function:

\[\begin{equation} \int_i(C_nCl_m|x_m,\sigma_i) = \frac{1}{\sigma_i\sqrt2\pi}\cdot e^{-(x_m-\%Cl_i)^2/2\sigma_i^2} \tag{2} \end{equation}\]

where the chlorination degree of the \(C_n\) standard \(i\) \((\%Cl_i)\) is the center of the peak, and the unknown variable \(\sigma_i\) is the standard deviation.

\(x_m\) is the \(\%Cl\) of \(C_nCl_m\), which can be calculated from the molecular formula:

\[\begin{align} x_m &\ {= \%Cl(C_nH_{2n+2-m}Cl_m)}\\ \\ &\ {= \frac{35.45\cdot m}{12.01\cdot n + 1.008\cdot (2n+2-m) + 35.45\cdot m}} \tag{3} \end{align}\]

So the concentration of \(C_nCl_m\) in the standard can be calculated as follows:

\[\begin{equation} concentration_i(C_nCl_m) = concentration_i(C_n) × C_nCl_m\%_i \tag{4} \end{equation}\]

Now the \(RF\) of \(C_nCl_m\) in the standard \(i\), or (1), can be rewritten as follows:

\[\begin{equation} RF_i(C_nCl_m) = \frac{area_i(C_nCl_m)}{concentration_i(C_n)\cdot \frac{\int_i(C_nCl_m|x_m,\sigma_i)}{\sum_{1}^m \int_i(C_nCl_m|x_m,\sigma_i)}} \tag{5} \end{equation}\]

where \(RF_i\) (\(C_nCl_m\)) only depends on the unknown \(\sigma_i\).

\(sigma_i\) is calculated by an iterative computation. The initial value of σi is set at 0.05 in each single chain standard, which is an empirical value for fast iteration. Then \(RF_i\) (\(C_nCl_m\)) is calculated using (5).
Pairwise comparisons are made between selected RF (e.g. \(C_{10}Cl_m\)) of two C10 standards (\(i\) and \(j\)) in the form of the square residual (\(SR\)):

\[\begin{equation} SR = \left[\frac{RF_i(C_{10}Clm) - RF_j(C_{10}Cl_m)}{\frac{RF_i(C_{10}Cl_m) + RF_j(C_{10}Cl_m)}{2}}\right]^2 \tag{6} \end{equation}\]
